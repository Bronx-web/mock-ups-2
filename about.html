Built to NZS 3604 - The New Zealand Timber-Framed Buildings Standard

NZS 3604 is the core building standard that virtually every residential house and low-rise building in New Zealand is constructed to. It sets the minimum requirements for foundations, bracing, timber framing, durability, weathertightness, and how brick veneer must be tied back to the structural frame.

When I say your brickwork is built to NZS 3604 (and the related veneer standards NZS 4229 & 4210), it means:

  • Correct cavity size and flashings so moisture can't get trapped behind the bricks
  • Stainless-steel veneer ties at the right spacing and embedded the correct depth
  • Proper control joints and movement gaps so the brick skin can expand/contract without cracking
  • Foundations and shelf angles designed for the exact wind and earthquake zone your house sits in
  • All detailing signed off as code-compliant - zero risk of failing council inspection

In short: your brickwork won't just look perfect on day one — it will still be perfect (and fully compliant) in 50 years, even after everything the New Zealand weather and the next big shake throws at it.
